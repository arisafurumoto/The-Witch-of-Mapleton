# Camellia

## Character Description

Camellia is the owner-chef of Mapleton’s restaurant and pub, and a romance candidate for Marigold. She is mature, elegant, capable, and quietly melancholic. She is loosely inspired by Gina from *Porco Rosso*: graceful, wistful, admired by others, and shaped by a deep past love.

She is known throughout Mapleton as someone who feeds people, listens more than she speaks, and keeps the village’s social heart alive. Although she is surrounded by customers and villagers every day, she keeps much of her own emotional life private.

Her story centres on grief, warmth, memory, and learning that loving again does not mean betraying the past.

## Personality

Camellia is calm, observant, dryly funny, and emotionally perceptive. She is warm in a practical way rather than an openly sentimental one. If someone is tired or upset, she is more likely to place food in front of them than ask direct questions.

She has a composed public face and rarely lets others see her unsettled. She is reliable, graceful under pressure, and quietly authoritative in her restaurant. However, she can also be guarded, stubbornly self-reliant, and reluctant to ask for help.

Camellia’s tenderness is real, but carefully controlled. She is slow to open herself romantically because she has already experienced love, loss, and the way both can reshape a life.

## Backstory

Camellia married very young and is now a widow. Her marriage was meaningful, but not simple. She loved her husband deeply, yet part of her own identity became smaller around the marriage, the restaurant, and the expectations placed on her.

After his death, Camellia continued running the restaurant and became one of Mapleton’s most dependable figures. The village relies on her warmth and stability, but privately she is still working out who she is without him.

Her grief is quiet and lived-in. It exists in routines, recipes, songs, objects, and habits she has not fully changed. She has moved forward in practical ways, but emotionally, part of her is still learning how to want a future again.

## Appearance

Camellia should look mature, elegant, and slightly wistful, while still practical enough to work in a busy kitchen.

She has deep auburn, dark chestnut, or wine-brown hair, usually pinned into a loose low bun or swept back gracefully. A few strands may fall loose when she is cooking. Her expression is calm and reflective, with warm but slightly tired eyes.

Her clothing combines understated elegance with restaurant practicality. She may wear a cream blouse with rolled sleeves, a dark fitted vest or bodice, a long burgundy, plum, or brown skirt, and a practical apron. Her palette should feel warm and rich: cream, wine red, dark brown, copper, muted rose, and deep green.

Subtle visual motifs include camellia flowers, copper cookware, candlelight, dark wood, cream linen, old recipes, and quiet music after closing.

# Sage

## Character Description

Sage is the owner of Mapleton’s plant shop and a gentle farming mentor for Marigold. He is a warm, kind, quietly observant elf who understands plants, soil, seasons, and the slow patience required to grow living things.

He helps Marigold learn about farming in a calm and practical way. His advice is never condescending. He teaches by noticing small details, explaining clearly, and giving plants and people room to grow at their own pace.

His story centres on self-discovery, quiet rebellion, and choosing a natural life over a perfectly controlled one.

## Personality

Sage is gentle, patient, kind, and softly humorous. He notices small things other people miss: a wilting leaf, a nervous customer, a tired expression, or a crop that is struggling before anyone else sees it.

He is soft-spoken but not weak. His gentleness is a deliberate choice, shaped by a past where control and expectation were disguised as care. He prefers to support rather than command.

Sage can be slightly awkward when praised and tends to hide his own needs behind helpfulness. He avoids confrontation when possible and still carries guilt about leaving his family. Beneath his calm exterior is someone still learning that he is allowed to choose an ordinary, happy life for himself.

## Backstory

Sage was born into a prominent traditional elven family and raised as the next heir. His life was shaped by etiquette, duty, reputation, lineage, and strict expectations. He was taught to be refined, impressive, controlled, and useful to his family’s legacy.

But Sage was drawn less to ceremony and more to the neglected corners of the garden: moss, weeds, seedlings, compost, roots, and imperfect living things. He wanted to touch dirt, grow messy things, and understand life from the soil up.

Eventually, the pressure of his family’s expectations became suffocating. He ran away to Mapleton, not because he hated his family, but because he needed to discover who he was outside the role they had prepared for him.

In Mapleton, he is building a quieter life around plants, practical kindness, and the freedom to grow in his own shape.

## Appearance

Sage should look soft, elegant, and slightly untidy, as though he comes from refinement but has chosen comfort and practicality.

He has silver hair that falls slightly into his eyes, usually tucked loosely behind one ear. His long elven ears are visible, though his hair may partly cover them. His face is gentle and thoughtful, with observant eyes and a calm expression.

He wears oversized soft linen button-downs in natural colours such as cream, sage green, faded blue, warm grey, or soft brown. His sleeves are often rolled up, revealing soil-stained forearms. His clothes are clean but not pristine, often marked by dirt, pollen, water, or crushed leaves.

Over his shirt, he wears a heavy canvas foraging apron filled with pruning shears, seed packets, twine, plant labels, gloves, and handwritten notes.

Subtle visual motifs include seed packets, potted herbs, clay pots, vines, damp soil, moss, twine, pruning shears, soft linen, and wildflowers growing where they were not meant to.

# Alder

## Character Description

Alder is Mapleton’s monster meat butcher and a potential romance candidate for Marigold. He is confident, affectionate, slightly chaotic, and unsettlingly calm. He handles the village’s more dangerous ingredients: monster meat, bones, rendered fat, hides, organs, and other materials most villagers prefer not to think about too closely.

He has a strong dislike for anything fake, mass-produced, sanitised, or too easy. To Alder, a thing only becomes meaningful when someone has touched it, risked something for it, or made it with care. He is rough around the edges, but not careless. His work is physical, bloody, and confronting, yet he treats it with a strange kind of respect.

He also has a side business making handmade soap from rendered fat from his butcher work. This gives him an odd contrast: blood, blades, and carcasses on one side; clean linen, herbal soap, and careful curing racks on the other.

Alder is also the main non-combat source for meat. Marigold can get meat by slaying
monsters in caves or by buying from Alder; she should not harvest meat from her own
ordinary farm animals or monster farm.

His route explores sincerity, violence, restraint, and whether someone rough can still be deeply caring. He is inspired by the anti-polished, anti-fake intensity of Tyler Durden from *Fight Club*, but should remain an original character suited to Mapleton’s cosy fantasy world.

## Personality

Alder is charismatic, direct, affectionate, and slightly dangerous in his energy. He speaks in a calm, philosophical monotone, even when saying something strange, tender, or provocative. He rarely sounds excited, but he is often amused.

He is not polite in the usual village way. He says what he means, dislikes social performance, and has little patience for people pretending to be kinder, richer, braver, or more refined than they are. Despite this, he is not cruel. He can be surprisingly gentle with frightened people, injured animals, and anyone who is trying sincerely.

Alder’s affection is physical and practical. He sharpens a tool for someone, carries something heavy without being asked, leaves soap at their door, or quietly teaches them how to hold a knife safely. He is protective, but his arc should examine the difference between protection and control.

He can be chaotic, provocative, and overly intense. He is drawn to difficulty and sometimes distrusts peace because it feels too easy. His growth should involve learning that restraint is not weakness, and gentleness does not make care less real.

## Backstory

Alder did not come from comfort. He learned early that polished things often hid ugly truths, while ugly work could be honest. Butchery suited him because it did not allow much pretending. Something was alive, then it was dead, and then it had to be used properly. Waste offended him more than blood.

At some point, Alder became known as someone willing to handle the dangerous, unpleasant, or morally uncomfortable work other people avoided. Monster meat butchery became his trade because it was difficult, risky, and necessary. He built a life around skill, physical discipline, and the belief that honest work leaves marks.

His soap-making began as a practical refusal to waste rendered fat, but over time it became one of his quietest forms of care. He experiments with herbs, ash, oils, flowers, and curing methods, though he rarely admits how much attention he gives it.

Alder’s past should remain somewhat rough and unresolved at this stage. The important foundation is that he has built his identity around sincerity, usefulness, and rejecting anything artificial. His emotional conflict comes from learning that being real does not always mean being harsh.

## Appearance

Alder should look rugged, confident, and slightly intimidating, but still attractive in a rough, charismatic way.

He has messy, short-cropped hair and a permanent smirk, as if he knows something amusing but will only explain it if asked directly. His expression is calm and unreadable, with eyes that feel observant rather than openly warm.

He wears a blood-stained leather apron over a threadbare but high-quality linen shirt. The shirt suggests he appreciates good materials, but not pristine presentation. His sleeves are often rolled up, and his hands and forearms show signs of hard work: small scars, calluses, stains, and careful knife discipline.

His design should contrast brutality with craft. He may carry butcher’s tools, twine, wrapped parcels, soap bars, or bundles of drying herbs. His colour palette should lean into dark leather, off-white linen, dried blood red, charcoal, bone, tallow cream, and muted herb green.

Subtle visual motifs include butcher knives, leather, linen, rendered fat, handmade soap, smoke, salt, bone, herbs, wrapped parcels, and clean objects made from violent work.

# Hinoki

## Character Description

Hinoki is Mapleton’s architect, builder, carpenter, and furniture maker. Through him, Marigold can buy furniture, building materials, and request house extensions. He is a jack of all trades who handles both the design and construction side of buildings, from drawing plans to cutting timber to finishing interiors.

He is kind, laid-back, curious, and a little scattered. He has inattentive ADHD, which shows through his many hobbies, unfinished projects, shifting interests, and tendency to become fascinated by whatever Marigold is currently doing. Farming, magic, monster slaying, crafting, cafe design, interior styling, toolmaking, and strange materials can all catch his attention.

Hinoki is relaxed in most areas of life, but surprisingly strict about design. He is very particular about his work and does not accept design changes from clients once he has decided something is right. He is easygoing as a person, but stubborn as a designer.

His story centres on creativity, distraction, perfectionism, unfinished ambition, and learning how to finish what matters without losing his curiosity.

## Personality

Hinoki is warm, friendly, laid-back, and highly curious. He is easy to talk to and rarely seems judgemental. He can drift between topics quickly, especially when something sparks a new idea. He may start a conversation about roof framing, become distracted by Marigold’s wand, then suddenly ask whether monster bone could be used as a structural material.

He is inattentive rather than hyperactive. He forgets appointments, misplaces tools, starts too many projects, and sometimes loses track of time while researching a niche idea. His workshop is full of half-built furniture, abandoned models, sketches, samples, and prototypes that made sense to him at 2 am.

Despite this, he is genuinely skilled. When he is focused, his work is precise, thoughtful, and beautiful. He notices proportion, light, texture, joinery, and how a space feels to live in. His relaxed manner hides a strong internal standard.

Hinoki is kind, but not endlessly flexible. When it comes to design, he can be quietly immovable. He believes buildings need coherence, and he dislikes making changes that weaken the idea. This can make him frustrating to work with, but it also gives his work a clear identity.

## Backstory

Hinoki grew up between influences. His mother is from the ancient village region, giving him a quiet connection to its materials, design traditions, craft methods, and sense of spatial beauty. This heritage shapes his eye, even if he does not always speak about it directly.

From a young age, Hinoki was interested in almost everything. He wanted to understand how houses stood up, how furniture was joined, how tools were made, how gardens changed a room, how light moved during the day, and why some spaces made people feel calm while others made them feel trapped.

Because of his inattentive ADHD, his interests often multiplied faster than he could finish them. He became skilled in many areas, but also developed a long trail of unfinished work. He is proud of what he can do, but quietly frustrated by how hard it can be to complete his own personal projects.

In Mapleton, he has become the person villagers rely on for repairs, furniture, renovations, and new construction. He is useful, well-liked, and constantly busy, but his own workshop remains crowded with ideas waiting for him to return to them.

## Appearance

Hinoki is tall and skinny, with a relaxed, slightly untidy elegance. He has shoulder-length wavy hair, usually worn in a half-up top knot. Loose strands often fall around his face when he is working.

He wears round glasses, which he frequently pushes up, misplaces, or forgets are already on his face. His expression is gentle and distracted, often looking as though he is thinking through three ideas at once.

His clothing should feel practical, soft, and design-conscious without being polished. He may wear loose work shirts, layered linen or cotton, cropped work trousers, tool belts, carpenter’s aprons, or casual jackets with rolled sleeves. His clothes often have sawdust, pencil marks, wood shavings, or smudges from paint, charcoal, or clay.

His design should combine architect, carpenter, and absent-minded creative. He may carry rolled plans, pencils, a ruler, wood samples, small tools, or a half-finished model piece.

Subtle visual motifs include timber, drafting paper, round glasses, measuring tools, half-built models, furniture sketches, sawdust, unfinished projects, ancient-region joinery details, and carefully designed spaces that look effortless.

# Linden

## Character Description

Linden is a business analyst for a large corporation, working remotely from Mapleton. He is tall, fit, sharply dressed, and intensely composed. Even though he works from a small village, he dresses every day in meticulous three-piece suits in muted tones, as if he is about to enter a boardroom rather than sit at a desk in a quiet rural town.

He speaks in a calm, flat monotone and values efficiency above almost everything. He dislikes wasted time, unclear instructions, emotional overreaction, and poorly designed systems. At first, he may seem detached or overly corporate, but he is more observant and considerate than he appears.

Linden is often one of the first people to notice when Marigold looks tired, overworked, or stretched too thin. He may not express concern warmly, but he will quietly offer a practical solution.

His route explores burnout, emotional restraint, discipline, and learning to value an inefficient but meaningful life. He is inspired by the composed, exhausted, highly competent energy of Nanami from *Jujutsu Kaisen*, but should remain an original character suited to Mapleton.

## Personality

Linden is calm, controlled, efficient, and emotionally restrained. He prefers clear plans, measurable outcomes, and structured routines. He does not enjoy chaos, but he is often very good at managing it.

He can seem blunt because he strips most emotion out of his speech. He rarely raises his voice, rarely shows panic, and rarely admits when something affects him. However, his restraint is not the same as indifference. He pays close attention to the people around him, especially when they are showing signs of fatigue or stress.

Linden is quietly caring in practical ways. He will reorganise a process, reduce someone’s workload, prepare a clean checklist, or tell Marigold to stop working because her output quality is visibly declining. His concern often sounds like criticism until the player knows him better.

He is competent, disciplined, and reliable, but also deeply tired. He has spent so long optimising his life around productivity that he struggles to understand rest, pleasure, and emotional honesty unless they have a clear purpose.

## Backstory

Linden works remotely for **Karazon**, a large online shopping corporation, and moved to Mapleton while continuing his role there. The move may have been framed as a lifestyle improvement, but beneath that is a quieter truth: he was exhausted.

His corporate life taught him to be useful, controlled, and efficient. He became very good at analysing problems, improving systems, managing stakeholders, and predicting risks. Over time, this also trained him to treat himself like a resource to be managed rather than a person with needs.

Karazon's CEO is originally from Mapleton, which gives Linden's work an odd local connection even though the company itself has no town storefront. Linden is not a shopkeeper in the village; he is a remote worker whose daily life is still shaped by corporate systems, metrics, and invisible online commerce.

Linden does not see Karazon as simply evil or good. It is a popular business, it is
convenient, and it is doing well. He understands why people use it. At the same time, he
likes the small, quaint town he lives in and loves Marigold's handcrafted work because
it has the human touch Karazon cannot reproduce.

Mapleton unsettles him because it runs on a different kind of logic. People stop to talk. Shops close early. Villagers help each other without calculating return on investment. Marigold may spend a whole day gathering flowers, rearranging furniture, helping someone emotionally, or chasing a strange magical curiosity with no obvious practical benefit.

At first, Linden sees this as inefficient. Gradually, he begins to understand that inefficiency is not always waste. Some things are valuable because they are slow, personal, beautiful, or unnecessary.

His arc centres on whether he can stop treating life as a problem to solve and start allowing himself to live it.

## Appearance

Linden is tall, fit, and sharply put together. He wears glasses and has a composed, unreadable expression. His posture is straight, controlled, and professional, even in casual village settings.

He dresses meticulously every day in three-piece suits, usually in muted tones such as charcoal, taupe, grey-brown, dark navy, olive-grey, or soft black. His shirts are crisp, his shoes are polished, and his accessories are minimal but precise. Even when working from home, he looks deliberately formal.

His appearance should contrast with Mapleton’s softer, messier village aesthetic. While other characters may wear aprons, linen, work clothes, or magical accessories, Linden looks like he stepped out of a corporate office and has not quite accepted that he lives in a cosy village.

Over time, his design could subtly soften without fully changing who he is: loosened tie, rolled sleeves, waistcoat without jacket, slightly less rigid hair, or a coat draped over his arm rather than perfectly worn.

Subtle visual motifs include glasses, muted suits, clean lines, pocket watches or slim wristwatches, notebooks, spreadsheets, fountain pens, neatly folded handkerchiefs, structured bags, and small signs of exhaustion hidden beneath immaculate presentation.

# Karazon CEO

## Character Description

The CEO of Karazon is an ambitious, money-loving business man originally from Mapleton.
He is still the current CEO when the game takes place. He built Karazon into a large
online shopping corporation with next-day delivery, free shipping, and no local
Mapleton storefront.

He should not feel like a simple villain. He genuinely understands convenience,
distribution, scale, and how to build a profitable business. His worldview is that if a
good thing can reach more people and make more money, then scaling it is sensible.

## Story Role

Karazon is a rival to Marigold's values rather than a direct magical competitor. It
does not sell magical handmade goods like hers. Its appeal is convenience: villagers can
order ordinary things quickly and cheaply without leaving home. The cost is that it has
no human touch, local memory, or sense of community.

Long-term, the CEO hears about Marigold's growing witch shop and offers to sell her
goods through Karazon. He promises to mass-produce them, give her a percentage, and make
her a millionaire. Marigold refuses because she cares about the love, intention, and
handmade care she puts into each craft, not just money.

## Connection To Anemone

Before he founded Karazon, the CEO met Anemone when he was young. He taught her about
capitalism, money, and running a business. Because mermaid society works through trade
and does not have the same concept of money, this encounter fascinated her and helped
inspire her to start her pond-side shop.

# Anemone

## Character Description

Anemone is a mermaid who secretly runs a pond-side shop in Mapleton. She is kind, playful, naive, and curious about life on land. Her shop sells fishing rods, fish, coral, pearls, shells, sea plants, and other ocean-related goods.

Although she is a mermaid, she is not against fishing. In this world, mermaids eat fish too, so she sees fishing as normal when done respectfully. She understands enough about land-dweller life to run her business, but there are still gaps in her knowledge. This can make her misunderstand ordinary village customs, prices, tools, idioms, or social expectations.

Anemone’s presence in Mapleton is secret because her people strongly distrust or hate humans. By running a business on land, she is breaking an important boundary. Her story centres on curiosity, innocence, secrecy, cultural prejudice, and choosing connection over inherited fear.

## Personality

Anemone is warm, playful, and openly affectionate. She is much less guarded than many of the other romance candidates and tends to express her feelings directly. She is excited by small land things that villagers take for granted, such as bread crusts, buttons, warm mugs, dry socks, wooden signs, or the idea of owning a chair.

She is naive, but not stupid. Her intelligence is shaped by a different world. She knows tides, fish behaviour, currents, shells, sea weather, underwater trade, and mermaid customs. Her mistakes usually come from assuming land society works more like ocean society.

She can be mischievous and teasing, especially when she feels safe. However, beneath her playful nature is a real fear of being discovered by her own people or rejected by land-dwellers. She wants to believe humans and mermaids can understand each other, but she does not fully grasp how difficult that may become.

## Backstory

Anemone comes from a mermaid society that strongly distrusts humans. She grew up hearing stories about humans as dangerous, greedy, careless, and destructive. Most mermaids avoid land-dwellers entirely, and some would consider peaceful contact a betrayal.

Despite this, Anemone became fascinated by the surface world. She collected objects that sank into the water, listened to stories carried by sailors and river spirits, and became curious about the village beyond the shore. Rather than seeing humans only as enemies, she began to wonder whether they were simply another kind of people.

Eventually, she found a way to open a secret shop connected to a dedicated pond in Mapleton. The pond acts as her safe point between water and land. It allows her to trade with villagers while hiding the full truth of her identity from those who would force her to return.

Her business is partly practical and partly an act of quiet rebellion. She wants to understand land life for herself, not only through the fears of her people.

Anemone's interest in business began when she once met the future CEO of Karazon while
he was young, before he started the company. He taught her about capitalism, money, and
running a business. This fascinated her because mermaid society is based on trade and
does not have the same concept of money. The encounter made human society feel strange,
structured, and exciting to her, and it eventually helped inspire her to start a shop of
her own.

This backstory should keep Anemone's innocence and curiosity intact. She is not a
corporate character; she is someone from a gift/trade culture trying to understand why
humans assign prices to things, keep accounts, advertise goods, and build businesses.

## Appearance

Anemone has long, wavy pink hair that flows like seaweed in water and falls in soft, heavy waves when dry. Her design should feel bright, pretty, aquatic, and slightly otherworldly.

Her eyes may be ocean blue, coral pink, violet, or pearl-toned, with a wide, curious expression. She should look kind and playful, with an open face that makes her emotions easy to read.

As a mermaid, she has aquatic features such as a shimmering tail, fin-like ears, pearl-like markings, soft scales, or translucent fin details. Her colour palette should feel oceanic and playful: pink, coral, pearl white, seafoam green, turquoise, shell cream, and soft gold.

When interacting with villagers, she may wear accessories gathered from both sea and land: pearl hair clips, coral jewellery, shell charms, ribbons, waterproof satchels, or a slightly awkward attempt at land fashion. Her shopkeeper look should make her feel like someone trying to understand human presentation while still clearly belonging to the sea.

Subtle visual motifs include anemone flowers, sea anemones, coral, pearls, shells, rippling water, fish, fishing rods, tide pools, sea glass, and a hidden pond connected to the ocean.

# Myrtle

## Character Description

Myrtle is Mapleton’s medical herbalist and village doctor. She treats illness, injuries, poison, exhaustion, magical side effects, fevers, sleep problems, and strange rashes using handmade medical potions, herbal remedies, poultices, teas, tinctures, breathwork, and body-awareness techniques.

Her methods look eccentric, but they are highly effective. Myrtle is not guessing or relying on superstition. She observes symptoms, tests ingredients, adjusts dosages, records outcomes, and remembers how each villager responds to treatment. Her work is medical in nature, but the village does not yet have the science to fully explain it.

Myrtle is spiritual, earthy, and unconventional. She meditates, practises yoga, believes in natural energy, and sees health as a connection between body, mind, plants, environment, and magic. This makes some villagers misunderstand her, even though they trust her results.

She is especially interested in Marigold’s magical knowledge. Myrtle believes magic could explain why some plants become stronger, why certain potions react strangely, and how healing can be made more precise. Her story centres on misunderstood intelligence, alternative medicine that genuinely works, and the difficulty of explaining advanced ideas in a world that is not ready for them.

## Personality

Myrtle is intelligent, vibrant, bubbly, and deeply curious. She speaks warmly and enthusiastically, especially when discussing plants, symptoms, potion reactions, or unusual magical illnesses. She has a caring presence that helps people feel safe when they are sick or frightened.

Although she seems whimsical, Myrtle becomes focused and practical when treating patients. She asks careful questions, checks symptoms, compares possible causes, and chooses treatments based on evidence from her own experience. She may describe illness through spiritual metaphors, but her decisions are grounded in observation.

Myrtle is open-minded and unconventional. She believes rest, emotion, environment, energy, diet, and magic can all affect health. To some villagers, this makes her sound strange. To Myrtle, it is simply a fuller way of understanding the body.

Her main flaw is that she often explains things in a way other people cannot follow. She may mix medical reasoning, plant knowledge, potion theory, and spiritual language in one sentence. She is usually right, but she has to learn that a good healer must also make patients understand and trust the treatment.

## Backstory

Myrtle grew up learning plant medicine from travelling herbalists, forest gatherers, midwives, shrine keepers, and village healers. She learned which roots reduce fever, which leaves settle the stomach, which flowers calm fear, which fungi are dangerous, and which ingredients should never be mixed.

When she later encountered formal medicine, she was frustrated by how quickly it dismissed old remedies and spiritual practices. Myrtle had seen too many of them work to ignore them. Instead of choosing one side, she built her own method: observe symptoms, test treatments, record results, and improve each remedy over time.

She settled in Mapleton because the area is rich in medicinal plants, rare mushrooms, mineral springs, and magical soil. At first, the villagers doubted her. Her clinic smelled strange, her potions looked odd, and her explanations were hard to understand.

Over time, Myrtle earned their trust by curing stubborn fevers, treating poisoned foragers, soothing magical burns, and helping exhausted villagers recover. Many still think she is strange, but they know her treatments work.

Her interest in Marigold begins when she realises Marigold’s magic may help explain the missing parts of her research. Myrtle wants to understand how spells affect the body, why enchanted plants behave differently, and whether magic can make healing safer and more reliable.

## Appearance

Myrtle has long, straight dark green hair that falls past her shoulders and frames her face naturally. Her hair has a relaxed 70s hippie look, with soft layers, a centre part, and a slightly wild herbalist quality rather than a polished style.

She wears a braided hippie-style headband across her forehead. The headband is made from leather or woven cord, with small beads, charms, and a central green jewel or wooden ornament. It is one of her strongest visual features and immediately gives her a free-spirited healer silhouette.

Her face is bright, warm, and expressive. She has kind green or hazel eyes, a wide friendly smile, and an alert expression that makes her seem both bubbly and highly observant. She should look welcoming, intelligent, and slightly mischievous.

Her outfit is earthy, layered, and handmade. She wears a loose cream blouse with wide sleeves, a dark moss-green vest, and a long warm brown skirt with subtle folk-style patterns. She also has a leather belt filled with potion bottles, herb pouches, small tools, and a notebook, making her look like someone who is always ready to treat a patient or gather plants.

Myrtle wears many natural accessories, including bead necklaces, leather bracelets, feather earrings, small charms, rings, and dangling cords. These details should make her feel spiritual and eccentric, but still practical enough to work as a village doctor.

Her colour palette should focus on dark green, moss green, cream, warm brown, faded gold, muted orange, and natural leather tones. Subtle visual motifs include dried herbs, potion bottles, notebooks, feathers, beads, myrtle leaves, green glass, mortar and pestle, herbal satchels, and handwritten medical notes.

# Dahlia

## Character Description

Dahlia owns a small clothes shop in Mapleton. She sells dresses, ribbons, hats, shoes, accessories, seasonal outfits, formalwear, and fashionable custom pieces for villagers who want to look their best.

She is sweet, bubbly, rich, popular, and very appearance-conscious. Dahlia loves beauty, fashion, compliments, trends, social events, and anything that makes life feel more sparkling. She has a Glinda-inspired presence: bright, glamorous, feminine, and charming, with a polished sweetness that makes her stand out in the village.

Although she has the energy of a typical queen bee, Dahlia is not intentionally cruel. She can be self-centred, dramatic, or out of touch, but she usually means well. Her story centres on growing beyond popularity, learning responsibility, and discovering that beauty has more value when it helps people feel confident rather than judged.

## Personality

Dahlia is cheerful, affectionate, and socially confident. She enjoys being admired and naturally becomes the centre of attention in most rooms. She remembers people’s outfits, notices small changes in their appearance, and gives compliments easily.

She can seem superficial because she cares so much about presentation. She worries about hair, clothes, colour matching, posture, first impressions, and whether something is “on trend.” However, she is aware of this flaw and wants to become more mature. She does not want to be seen as just a pretty, rich girl who only cares about herself.

Dahlia is caring, but sometimes clumsy with her kindness. She might give someone a makeover when they only needed comfort, or assume a new outfit can fix a deeper problem. Her intentions are usually good, but she still has to learn when to listen instead of decorating the situation.

Owning her clothes shop is her first serious attempt to become independent. She is eager to learn, but she makes mistakes with pricing, stock, budgeting, customer needs, and practical repairs. She is embarrassed when she fails, but she tries again quickly.

## Backstory

Dahlia grew up in a wealthy family and was used to being admired. She had beautiful clothes, social invitations, expensive gifts, and people who expected her to be charming. For most of her life, being pretty, popular, and pleasant was enough.

As she got older, Dahlia began to realise that admiration was not the same as respect. Some villagers liked her, but did not take her seriously. Others assumed she was spoiled, shallow, or unable to work hard. This hurt her more than she admitted.

## Personality

Oak is confident, warm, witty, and physically energetic. He has an easy grin, a dry sense of humour, and a habit of making jokes when situations become dangerous. He is not fearless, but he acts quickly under pressure and usually worries about the consequences afterwards.

He is curious to a fault. Ancient doors, sealed chambers, strange symbols, buried machines, old bones, and magical cave formations all pull him in immediately. He can be responsible when other people are at risk, but when he is alone, his judgement becomes looser.

Oak is loyal and protective. Once he trusts someone, he shows up for them without making a performance of it. He will carry supplies, walk someone home after dark, distract a monster, or help Marigold explore dangerous areas even when he pretends he only came along for “research purposes.”

He is more intelligent than his rugged appearance suggests. He can read old maps, identify construction methods, interpret worn carvings, recognise burial customs, and notice when a ruin has been altered by magic. However, he dislikes academic arrogance and prefers practical knowledge over status.

His main flaw is recklessness. He is used to surviving danger, so he sometimes underestimates how much his choices affect others. His growth involves learning that bravery is not the same as treating his own safety as disposable.

## Backstory

He trained as an archaeologist, but never fit neatly into formal academic life. He was too physical, too impatient with bureaucracy, and too willing to follow a lead before permission arrived. He became known as a talented field researcher who could handle difficult terrain, unstable ruins, dangerous wildlife, and magical hazards that more cautious scholars avoided.

Oak came to Mapleton after finding references to old cave structures beneath the area. What began as a normal research trip became more serious when he discovered signs that the caves may be connected to Mapleton’s lost magic. He now believes the village’s history is incomplete, and that something important was buried, sealed, or deliberately forgotten.

He rents a small cottage in the village, though it often looks more like a storage room than a home. It is filled with maps, stone rubbings, broken pottery, ropes, lanterns, field journals, muddy boots, and half-cleaned artefacts. He tells people he will organise it “after the next survey,” but the next survey always comes first.

# Aoi

## Character Description

**Aoi** is a travelling shamisen player from the ancient region who visits Mapleton once a week to perform at the village pub. Beautiful, polite, and openly flirtatious, he has a graceful charm that makes him difficult to ignore and even harder to read. His performances are atmospheric and precise, often played while wearing a fox mask that turns him into something distant, theatrical, and almost otherworldly.

Aoi composes music from the sounds most people overlook: rain on rooftops, birdsong at dawn, forest winds, shop bells, footsteps on old wood, restless spirits, and the strange warmth of Marigold’s magic. He treats the world as if it is always singing quietly beneath ordinary life.

To most villagers, Aoi is captivating company. He smiles easily, speaks beautifully, and knows exactly how to make someone feel seen. Yet beneath the charm is a man who keeps his true feelings carefully hidden.

## Personality

Aoi is elegant, perceptive, charming, and socially effortless. He is openly flirtatious, but rarely careless. His compliments are precise, his smiles are intentional, and his attention can feel intoxicating because he knows exactly when to give it and when to withdraw it.

He is deeply aware of his beauty and charm, and sometimes uses both to influence others. He is not cruel, but he can be manipulative when he wants control of a situation. Aoi prefers emotional ambiguity, allowing people to wonder whether he is sincere, teasing, or performing.

Beneath this polished surface is a guarded perfectionist who distrusts easy affection. Past relationships taught him that people can become attached to the image of him: his face, his voice, his elegance, his mystery. Because of this, Aoi avoids deep attachments, even while inviting admiration. He wants to be truly known, but fears that once the performance ends, there may be nothing left that someone wants to stay for.

## Backstory

Aoi was raised in the ancient region, where music, ritual, and performance are closely tied to seasonal festivals and spirit traditions. From a young age, he was praised for his beauty, discipline, and natural ability with the shamisen. Over time, he learned that charm could open doors, silence criticism, and keep people at a safe distance.

As he became known as a performer, admiration followed him everywhere. He had relationships with women who adored him intensely, but he often felt they were in love with his beauty, mystery, and public persona rather than his true self. Their obsession made intimacy feel less like being understood and more like being possessed.

Travelling became his solution. By never staying too long, Aoi could enjoy affection without becoming trapped by it. His fox mask became part of that distance: a beautiful surface people could admire without reaching the man beneath it. Mapleton unsettles him because, for the first time in years, he finds himself wanting to return.

## Appearance

Aoi is a beautiful young man with refined features, calm eyes, and a soft, knowing smile. His long, straight hair is a deep violet-black, worn loose and neatly groomed, falling past his shoulders with smooth bangs that frame his face. He has an elegant, slightly androgynous beauty, but his presence is composed rather than delicate.

He wears a clean Japanese-inspired outfit suited to a travelling performer: an ivory inner robe, a dark indigo-purple haori cut around knee height, a simple obi, and subtle red cord accents. His clothing is graceful and understated, with no heavy ornamentation or floral patterning. The effect is polished, theatrical, and controlled.

Aoi carries a shamisen as his main instrument and performs with a white fox mask marked in red. When worn, the mask changes his presence completely, making his music feel more distant, mysterious, and ritualistic. Offstage, he may hold the mask casually, as if reminding others that even his bare face might be another performance.

# Ayame

## Character Description

Ayame is the scroll keeper of the ancient Japanese region, a quiet and highly respected librarian who manages one of the region’s oldest archives. Her archive sits near the heart of the old town, close to shrines, stone paths, mossy gardens, and traditional wooden buildings. It is not a normal library. It is a protected storehouse of scrolls, records, recipes, spirit contracts, family histories, old maps, forgotten techniques, and magical knowledge that has survived for generations.

When Marigold begins exploring the Japanese region, she finds torn scroll fragments hidden in ruins, shrines, caves, abandoned houses, forest paths, and quest locations. These fragments can be brought to Ayame, who carefully studies and reconstructs them. Through Ayame’s work, the player can unlock new crafting recipes, potion formulas, regional lore, hidden quests, shrine puzzles, magical techniques, and deeper knowledge about the history of the world.

Ayame’s role is slower and more reflective than many other villagers. She is not a merchant who loudly advertises her services. Players are drawn to her because the archive feels important, mysterious, and quietly sacred. She represents memory, preservation, patience, and the idea that forgotten knowledge can still shape the present. Her romance route should feel gradual, intimate, and intellectual, built through trust, shared discoveries, and the careful restoration of lost stories.

## Personality

Ayame is calm, precise, disciplined, and deeply observant. She chooses her words carefully and rarely speaks without purpose. At first, she may seem distant, formal, or difficult to read, especially compared to warmer and more openly expressive characters. This is not because she is unkind. Ayame has spent most of her life around fragile records, old magic, and traditions that demand patience. She instinctively treats people, stories, and objects with the same careful attention.

She dislikes carelessness. If Marigold brings her a damaged scroll fragment, Ayame may gently but firmly explain how easily old paper can be destroyed. She is especially sensitive to people treating the past as decoration, superstition, or novelty. To Ayame, every scroll contains someone’s labour, belief, fear, hope, or mistake. Even a small fragment deserves respect.

Beneath her reserved manner, Ayame is quietly kind. She notices when Marigold is exhausted, confused, or carrying too much responsibility. Instead of offering dramatic comfort, she may prepare tea, leave a relevant note, or guide Marigold toward an answer without making her feel foolish. Her humour is dry and subtle. Romantically, she opens up slowly. Her affection is shown through trust: allowing Marigold into restricted archive rooms, sharing personal interpretations of old texts, and eventually admitting that some things are worth preserving because they are still alive in the present.

## Backstory

Ayame was born and raised in the ancient Japanese region, where her family or teachers served as caretakers of written history for generations. From childhood, she was taught how to handle scrolls, repair paper, identify ink, recognise seals, read archaic phrasing, and understand the spiritual weight of old records. While other children played freely in the streets, Ayame spent long afternoons learning how to preserve documents without disturbing the magic embedded in them.

Her talent became obvious early. She could identify damaged scrolls by texture, reconstruct missing phrases through context, and sense when a document had been altered or sealed. Because of this, she was entrusted with more responsibility than most people her age. That responsibility shaped her into someone disciplined and self-contained, but it also isolated her. Ayame learned to be useful before she learned how to be emotionally open.

Many important scrolls in the region were damaged, scattered, or deliberately hidden during past conflicts, disasters, or spiritual disturbances. Ayame made it her life’s work to restore them. When Marigold arrives and begins finding fragments beyond the archive’s reach, Ayame is both cautious and intrigued. Marigold represents something Ayame cannot achieve alone: movement, instinct, risk, and direct contact with the living world outside the archive. Through their relationship, Ayame begins to understand that preserving history does not mean keeping it locked away forever.

## Appearance

Ayame has a refined traditional appearance with a restrained scholarly elegance. She has a short bob haircut with heavy bangs, creating a clear silhouette that should work well for both portrait art and pixel sprites. Her expression is usually composed and thoughtful, with calm, observant eyes. She should look intelligent and graceful, but not overly ornamental or fragile. Her design should communicate that she works with scrolls, ink, tea, paper, and old magic.

Her clothing is based on a classic kimono rather than a hakama-style outfit. The kimono should feel traditional, practical, and elegant, suitable for someone who spends long hours in an archive. Since this is for a pixel game, the design should avoid dense tiny patterns. A few larger, simple floral motifs are enough. The flowers should not cover the whole outfit. Subtle placement on the sleeve, hem, or obi would be better than a busy all-over print.

Her colour palette should feel calm and slightly mature: indigo, muted plum, soft cream, dusty rose, deep brown, faded teal, or subdued violet would all suit her. She does not wear a haori jacket in the current direction. Accessories should be minimal: perhaps a simple hairpin, a corded obi, a protective paper charm, or an ink brush tucked nearby. In concept art, she can be shown with a Japanese-style teacup, scroll fragments, or archival tools, but her hands should remain visible and expressive.

She understands that clothing changes how people carry themselves. To her, a dress, coat, ribbon, or pair of boots is not just decoration. In Mapleton, Petunia brings colour, confidence, and spectacle. She is the character who makes the village feel more socially alive. She knows everyone’s measurements, favourite colours, secret insecurities, and festival plans. As a romance candidate, she offers a more overtly charming and flirtatious route than Ayame, but her deeper arc is about being loved when she is not perfectly composed, dressed beautifully, or performing for others.

## Personality

Petunia is warm, dazzling, dramatic, and socially intelligent. She enters a room as though the lighting has been arranged for her, and she has a talent for making even ordinary errands feel like an occasion. She speaks with confidence, compliments people easily, and often seems to know exactly what someone needs to hear. Her default mode is bright and encouraging, but not naive. Petunia reads people very well, especially when they are trying to hide discomfort behind politeness.

She can be playful and flirtatious, particularly with Marigold. Her affection is more visible than Ayame’s: lingering compliments, teasing remarks, carefully chosen gifts, and small adjustments to Marigold’s outfit before an important event. She enjoys beauty, but she is also precise and hardworking. Behind the sparkling exterior is someone who spends long hours sewing, altering, dyeing, ordering materials, and managing customers with very specific demands.

Petunia’s flaw is that she struggles to be seen as imperfect. She is used to being admired, but admiration can become a cage. She may hide exhaustion, disappointment, jealousy, or loneliness behind a polished smile. Her romance route should slowly reveal the contrast between her public glamour and her private vulnerability. She wants to be adored, but what she needs is to be understood without having to perform.

## Backstory

Petunia grew up surrounded by beauty, etiquette, theatre, and expectation. She may have come from a family of dressmakers, performers, magical stylists, or social figures who believed appearance was a language that had to be mastered. From a young age, she learned how to match colours, alter silhouettes, read posture, and understand what people wanted to become when they looked in the mirror. She had talent early, but she was also praised most when she was graceful, charming, and visually perfect.

That praise shaped her. Petunia learned that beauty could open doors, calm arguments, attract customers, and make people listen. She also learned that people often stopped looking deeper once they were dazzled. Before settling in Mapleton, she may have worked in a larger town where fashion was competitive, status-driven, and emotionally exhausting. Her designs were admired, but she felt increasingly like part of the display.

Mapleton gave her a different kind of opportunity. Instead of dressing people for status, she could dress them for real lives: festivals, first dates, shop openings, rainy walks, magical adventures, and quiet confidence. Her boutique became her attempt to create beauty without cruelty. Through Marigold, Petunia’s arc can explore the difference between being admired as an image and being loved as a whole person.
